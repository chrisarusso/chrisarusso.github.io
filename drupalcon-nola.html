Total value of ownership
Look at the CMS landscape as a whole, understand the value we're looking to deliver, understand how Drupal is able to deliver that value (with our help) I am biased, but have attempted to be impartial
Total cost of ownership (TCO) is a financial estimate intended to help buyers and owners determine the direct and indirect costs of a product or system.

- the Wikipedias

Total value of ownership

  • Defined by each organization
  • Admittedly difficult to measure
More specifically each org's mission If they’re an e-commerce retailer, perhaps it’s sales If they’re a mission based non-profit, perhaps it’s reach to a given audience If they’re a publisher, perhaps an advanced editorial workflow is most important Your job is to really understand that value they're looking to achieve with your help

Where does Drupal not make sense in?

Drupal MAY not be a good solution if you’re Inertia! What’s the current site in? What are devs used to? What are admins used to? Client is not expecting support / new updates Shoe-horn into Drupal is damaging to the experience broadly. Client that isn’t ready for maintenance upgrades Answer - Jekyll github hosted Client that expects their team to be able to develop against it, but has no Drupal experience That’s why there are so many rescue projects out there. Might be good for your team for a bit, but bad for product in general. Client that doesn’t need to update things over time, isn’t interested in the latest and greatest tech. Client who is familiar and likes another CMS. Sometimes this is okay, but dragging them away and changing things up, you better have a real benefit to it vs. you’re familiar with developing in it. Client who have developers who are .net or the like
